## Usage Tips for Dungeon Maps

### Room Labeling
Consider including:
- Room numbers (1, 2, 3...)
- Room purposes (Guard Room, Library)
- Brief descriptions in legend

### Door Types
Indicate:
- Regular doors (simple line)
- Secret doors (marked specially)
- Locked doors (marked with key symbol)
- Barred/blocked doors
- Portcullis or gates

### Scale Considerations
- Small dungeons: Show entire level
- Large dungeons: Break into sections
- Multi-level: Create separate maps with connection notes

### Features to Mark
- Stairs (up/down arrows)
- Traps (for GM reference)
- Secret doors
- Treasure locations (for GM)
- Monster lairs
- Important objects

### Style Choices
- **Classic:** Black and white, simple, functional
- **Detailed:** Textured walls/floors, furniture shown
- **Isometric:** 3D view, beautiful but less precise
- **Architectural:** Clean lines, measured, professional
